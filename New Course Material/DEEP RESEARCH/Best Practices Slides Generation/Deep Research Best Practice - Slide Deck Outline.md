# Deep Research Best Practice

## Slide 1

**Title:** Deep research is not just better search

**Subtitle:** It investigates, cross-checks and synthesises

- Deep research breaks a question into sub-questions, gathers evidence, filters sources and builds a structured view.
- It is best used for sector scans, target research, management work and market outlooks.
- The output should inform judgement, not replace it.
- In private equity, the value is speed, coverage and a cleaner first-pass view.

## Slide 2

**Title:** When to use it

**Subtitle:** Use it for synthesis, not simple look-ups

- Use deep research when the question needs many sources, cross-checking and a clear conclusion.
- Do not use it for requests that can be answered in a short paragraph.
- One coherent objective per run is better than several unrelated questions.
- Define geography, timeframe, sub-sector and decision before you start.

## Slide 3

**Title:** Recommended workflow

**Subtitle:** Scope first, evidence second, judgement last

- Start with the decision: screen, prioritise, proceed to diligence or prepare IC discussion.
- Run public-web research first; keep sensitive or proprietary material in a separate phase where possible.
- Ask the model to separate `Facts`, `Assumptions`, `Inferences` and `Conclusions`.
- Manually verify the claims that could change appetite, valuation or diligence direction.

## Slide 4

**Title:** Prompting best practice

**Subtitle:** Specific prompts produce better research

- Ask analytical questions, not broad summaries.
- Set evaluation criteria: what should be compared, tested or explained.
- Set the output structure up front: summary, findings, evidence, gaps, next checks.
- Use `RCTIO`: `Role`, `Context`, `Task`, `Instructions`, `Output`.

## Slide 5

**Title:** Reducing hallucinations

**Subtitle:** Design the workflow for verification

- Prioritise filings, company websites, transcripts, regulators and reputable industry sources.
- Treat important numbers as verification tasks, not as final answers.
- Label support as `Direct confirmation`, `Triangulation` or `Contextual support`.
- Flag uncertainty, stale data, missing evidence and conflicting claims explicitly.

## Slide 6

**Title:** Which model when

**Subtitle:** Match the model to the source environment

**Tab-delimited table for Keynote**

```text
Model	Best use	Watch-out
ChatGPT / OpenAI deep research	Broad outside-in research across the web and connected sources	Needs a well-scoped brief up front
Claude	Work from a defined document pack	Less suited to broad live-web exploration
Gemini Deep Research	Large-scope synthesis across files plus web	Can become too broad without tight structure
Grok / DeepSearch	Real-time news, market pulse and X-linked narrative tracking	Verify investment-critical claims from primary sources
```

## Optional closing slide

**Title:** Practical rules for the deal team

**Subtitle:** Keep the workflow disciplined

- Ask one clear question at a time.
- Define scope and evaluation criteria before the run starts.
- Prefer primary and high-trust sources.
- Separate facts from interpretation.
- Verify the claims that matter most to the investment case.
