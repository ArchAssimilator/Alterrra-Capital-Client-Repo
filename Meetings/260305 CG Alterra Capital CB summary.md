## Alterra Capital Business Overview

Alterra is a ~22-person private equity firm. Their core workflow spans the full investment lifecycle:

1. **Deal Sourcing & Evaluation** - Receive business plans, present to Investment Committee (IC) in PowerPoint format. Kill ~50% of deals immediately, ~80% overall. Already using ChatGPT to generate initial 4-page presentations (~70% there in text, but not yet in PowerPoint format).

2. **Due Diligence** - Process financials from Excel/PDF, run analytics, iterate on returns and forecasts. Use third-party consultants (Bain, BCG) for industry reports. Already using ChatGPT for preliminary industry research.

3. **Legal Document Review** - Hire top-tier legal firms (White & Case, Webber Wentzel). Already using AI to assess legal documents, have started training on NDAs and simpler docs. Post-investment, they create ChatGPT projects per portfolio company for legal and commercial documents.

4. **Portfolio Management** - Board meetings, quarterly reports from portfolio companies, interaction with management teams. Quarterly investor reporting consumes significant junior staff time and is a prime automation candidate.

5. **Exit/Sales Processes** - Preparing materials with banks/advisors, financial/tax/legal workstreams.

6. **Back/Middle Office** - Invoices, travel, expenses, HR (hiring/firing) - standard operations.

---

## Current AI Usage & Pain Points

- **ChatGPT** is their primary tool; they find it superior for current/real-time information and industry research
- **Claude** criticised for outdated knowledge cutoff; however, recognised as better for Microsoft product integration (especially PowerPoint generation)
- Text output from AI is good, but **converting to PowerPoint remains a gap**
- Considered switching from PowerPoint to Word as their presentation format but resistance from "middle-aged" partners
- Already using AI for: reading consultant reports, summarising key takeaways, finding errors, legal document Q&A

## Masterclass Structure Discussion

### Paul's Recall of Previous Course Structure:
1. What is AI? (Claude vs ChatGPT, strengths of each platform)
2. Prompting (natural language programming, better questions = better answers)
3. Deep Research (use cases for investment diligence)
4. Agents (repetitive tasks - e.g., corporate handbook queries, NDA review)
5. Vibe Coding (most enjoyable part of previous course)

### Paul's Preferences for This Course:
- **Target level: Medium to Advanced** - "If they haven't gotten through basic, they should find the exit door"
- Wants to **pick specific automation use cases** and build them live (e.g., quarterly reporting, ESG reporting)
- Has an **Excel list of projects** he wants staff to automate but they keep procrastinating
- Wants exercises that create something reusable: "spend 1 hour, save 5 hours going forward"
- Keep the **vibe coding fun session** - it gets people excited and drives ongoing adoption
- Suggested **NDA review agent** as a practical exercise: feed in rules (no indemnity, max 2-year terms, etc.), precedent NDAs, then query new NDAs against the rulebook

### Team's (RJ/Chris) Recommendations:
- Still cover fundamentals (settings, memory, custom instructions, context windows) - even experienced users have gaps
- Use a **single investment use case** (e.g., business plan review) threaded through multiple exercises
- Explain **context rot** and why token management matters for consulting-grade output
- Show **deep research as a verification tool** (counterintuitive but effective for checking final documents)
- Address **Excel/spreadsheet limitations** with AI honestly - complex multi-tab models don't work well, but simple table extraction is viable
- **Advanced data analysis/statistics** section not relevant for Alterra - can be dropped
