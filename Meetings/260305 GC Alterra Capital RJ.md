# Meeting Notes: GC Alterra Capital

As recorded by MS Teams

## Current Workflow and Automation Opportunities at Altera

Paul Maasdorp and Bruce Steen provided a detailed overview of Altera's internal processes, highlighting areas such as capital raising, opportunity evaluation, legal document management, portfolio company reporting, and back-office operations, and discussed where automation and AI could drive efficiency and cost savings.

- **Capital Raising Automation**: Paul described the repetitive nature of investor Q&A, PowerPoint preparation, and meeting scheduling during capital raising, noting that most questions are predictable and could be answered from a pre-built library, and that automating these tasks could significantly reduce the team's workload.

- **Opportunity Evaluation Process**: The team receives lengthy documents for new opportunities, processes financials in Excel, and creates internal presentations for investment committee review; Paul noted that ChatGPT has been trained to generate initial presentations, but further automation is needed for more advanced stages and integration with PowerPoint.

- **Legal Document Handling**: Paul and Bruce Steen explained that legal documents are managed by external firms, but AI is already being used to review and summarise NDAs and other contracts, with the aim of further automating legal review and negotiation processes.

- **Portfolio Company Reporting**: Quarterly reporting for investors involves extracting and reformatting financial data from portfolio companies, a process that currently consumes significant junior staff time and is identified as a prime candidate for automation.

- **Back and Middle Office Operations**: Bruce Steen added that Altera's back and middle office functions, such as invoice payments, travel expenses, and HR tasks, are similar to other organisations and could also benefit from automation.

## AI Training Needs and Course Structure Discussion

Chris, Rutger-Jan, Paul, and Bruce Steen discussed the optimal focus and structure for an upcoming AI training session, debating whether to prioritise basic AI concepts, medium-to-advanced skills, or hands-on automation projects tailored to Altera's workflow.

- **Training Content Level**: Paul advocated for a medium-to-advanced training focus, suggesting that basic AI concepts could be covered in pre-reading, while Bruce Steen and Rutger-Jan emphasised the need to level-set participants' knowledge to ensure no one is left behind.

- **Course Structure and Modules**: Rutger-Jan outlined a modular approach, starting with AI fundamentals, prompt engineering, deep research, and agent-based automation, with flexibility to adapt based on participant needs and prior experience.

- **Practical Use Cases and Group Projects**: Paul and Chris discussed incorporating real Altera use cases, such as automating quarterly reporting or NDA review, into group projects to ensure practical, directly applicable learning outcomes.

- **Vibe Coding and Engagement**: Bruce Steen and Chris debated the value of including a 'vibe coding' session—an engaging, creative coding exercise—to boost participant interest and learning, with consensus that both fun and business-relevant projects could be accommodated over the two-day course.

## Technical Challenges and Limitations of AI Tools

Paul, Bruce Steen, Chris, and Rutger-Jan examined the current limitations of AI tools in handling complex data tasks, such as extracting and processing financial data from PDFs and spreadsheets, and discussed strategies to overcome these challenges in Altera's context.

- **Spreadsheet and Data Extraction Limitations**: Rutger-Jan and Bruce Steen highlighted the difficulty AI tools face in extracting actionable insights from complex, multi-dimensional spreadsheets, noting that current solutions often require intermediate steps and data reformatting.

- **PDF to Excel Workflow**: Bruce Steen described the manual process of transferring financial data from PDF documents into Excel, validating the data, and then overlaying Altera's own assumptions, a process that is time-consuming and not yet fully automated by AI.

- **AI Model Context and Memory Management**: Chris and Rutger-Jan explained the importance of understanding AI model context windows, memory features, and prompt structuring to avoid 'context rot' and ensure reliable outputs, sharing best practices for managing project-specific memory and settings.

- **Tool Selection and Integration**: Paul and the team discussed the relative strengths of ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini for different tasks, noting that ChatGPT is preferred for up-to-date industry research, while integration with Microsoft products and advanced spreadsheet handling remain areas for improvement.

## Logistics and Planning for AI Training Sessions

Rutger-Jan, Paul, Bruce Steen, and Chris coordinated the logistics for upcoming AI training sessions in Nairobi and Johannesburg, including participant lists, session content, venue arrangements, and cost-sharing with potential partner organisations.

- **Session Scheduling and Venues**: The team confirmed training dates, discussed using Altera's offices in Nairobi, and reviewed available infrastructure such as TVs and projectors for the sessions.

- **Participant Coordination**: Paul and Bruce Steen agreed to provide lists of attendees to help tailor the training, and Paul explored the possibility of inviting other private equity groups to share costs and maximise value.

- **Pricing and Group Size**: Rutger-Jan clarified the session pricing, VAT considerations, and recommended an optimal group size of up to 12 participants for effective learning and cost efficiency.
