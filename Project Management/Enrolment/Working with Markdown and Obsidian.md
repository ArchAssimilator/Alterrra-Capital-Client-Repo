## 1. Why Markdown Matters

Markdown is a lightweight markup language that transforms how you work with text. Unlike proprietary formats locked into specific applications, Markdown files are plain text with simple formatting syntax that any text editor can open.

**Key benefits for professionals:**

- **Future-proof documentation** – Your notes from 2024 will be readable in 2044, regardless of which companies or applications survive
- **AI-ready content** – Large language models work exceptionally well with Markdown's clean structure, making it ideal for AI-assisted workflows
- **Universal compatibility** – Write once, use everywhere: documentation, emails, presentations, web content, or reports
- **Version control friendly** – Perfect for tracking changes in Git or other version control systems
- **Distraction-free writing** – Focus on content, not formatting buttons and ribbons

Markdown strikes the perfect balance: simple enough to write quickly, structured enough to convert into any format you need.

## 2. The Power of Local, Accessible Files

Storing your Markdown files directly on your hard drive (with optional cloud synchronisation) gives you control and flexibility that web-based applications cannot match.

**Why this approach wins:**

- **Application independence** – Switch between Obsidian, VS Code, Typora, or any text editor without conversion or export hassles
- **Offline access** – Your knowledge base works on aeroplanes, in areas with poor connectivity, or when cloud services are down
- **Data sovereignty** – You control where your intellectual property lives, who can access it, and how it's backed up
- **Speed and reliability** – No waiting for servers to respond; your files load instantly
- **Integration flexibility** – Use automation tools, scripts, or AI applications to process your files directly
- **Sync on your terms** – Choose Dropbox, OneDrive, Google Drive, iCloud, or your own synchronisation solution

This architecture ensures your knowledge system adapts to your needs, not the other way round.

## 3. Why Obsidian Excels

Obsidian is a powerful Markdown editor and knowledge management tool built on a simple principle: your files remain yours, stored locally in plain Markdown format.

**Obsidian's distinctive advantages:**

- **Bidirectional linking** – Create connections between notes with `[[wiki-style links]]` that build your personal knowledge graph
- **Graph view** – Visualise how your ideas connect, revealing patterns and relationships you might otherwise miss
- **Local-first architecture** – Fast, private, and works entirely offline whilst remaining compatible with your existing file structure
- **Extensive plugin ecosystem** – Customise your workspace with community-built extensions for tasks, calendars, diagrams, and more
- **Live preview** – See formatted output as you type, without switching between edit and preview modes
- **Powerful search** – Find anything across thousands of notes instantly, with support for regular expressions and Boolean operators
- **Free for personal use** – Full-featured application without subscription fees for individual users

Obsidian transforms your collection of Markdown files into an interconnected thinking environment.

## 4. Understanding Obsidian's Idiosyncrasies

Obsidian works differently from traditional applications. Understanding these differences prevents confusion and helps you work more effectively.

### Forget About "Vaults" Initially

Obsidian uses the term "vault" to describe what is simply **a folder on your computer**. Don't let this terminology intimidate you. When Obsidian asks you to "open a vault", it's asking you to point it at a folder containing (or ready to contain) your Markdown files. That's it.

**In practice:** Select any folder on your hard drive, and Obsidian treats it as your working space. No special setup required.

### IMPORTANT: Don't "Create New Vault"

When you first launch Obsidian, you'll see a startup screen with three options:

1. **Create new vault** – "Create a new Obsidian vault under a folder" (with a purple "Create" button)
2. **Open folder as vault** – "Choose an existing folder of Markdown files" (with a grey "Open" button)
3. **Open vault from Obsidian Sync** – "Set up a synced vault with existing remote vault" (with a grey "Setup" button)

**Warning: Do NOT click "Create new vault".** This option adds unnecessary complexity for beginners and creates confusion about where your files are stored. Ignore this option entirely during the master class.

**Instead, ALWAYS use "Open folder as vault":**

1. Click the grey "Open" button next to "Open folder as vault"
2. Navigate to any existing folder on your computer (or create a new empty folder using your file explorer first)
3. Select that folder
4. Click "Select Folder" or "Open"
5. Start working immediately

This approach keeps things simple: you know exactly where your files are, you can access them with any application, and you maintain full control over your file structure. The folder you select becomes your "vault" – nothing more complicated than that.

### You Open Folders, Not Files

Unlike Word or most applications where you open individual documents, **Obsidian opens an entire folder** and displays all Markdown files within it in the sidebar. This is a feature, not a limitation.

**Why this matters:** Once you've opened a folder in Obsidian, every `.md` file in that folder (and its subfolders) becomes immediately accessible. Add a new Markdown file to the folder outside Obsidian? It appears in your sidebar instantly. No need to import, refresh, or reopen anything.

### Create Everything Within Obsidian

You don't need to leave Obsidian to organise your files. The application provides full file and folder management capabilities.

**What you can do inside Obsidian:**

- Create new Markdown files (right-click in sidebar → "New note")
- Create folder structures (right-click in sidebar → "New folder")
- Move files between folders (drag and drop)
- Rename files and folders
- Delete files

**Best practice:** Build your entire folder structure and create all your notes within Obsidian. This keeps your workflow streamlined and ensures all files are properly indexed.

### Files Appear Automatically

When working with colleagues or synchronising across devices, new files appear in Obsidian's sidebar as soon as they're added to your folder. No manual refresh needed. This makes collaborative work and multi-device workflows seamless.

### Start Simple

Don't feel pressured to use every feature immediately. Start by:

1. Opening a folder (creating one if needed)
2. Creating a few Markdown notes
3. Writing in plain Markdown syntax
4. Exploring links between notes with `[[note name]]` syntax

Advanced features like plugins, templates, and graph view can wait until you're comfortable with the basics.

---

**Remember:** Obsidian is a tool to enhance your thinking and note-taking, not a complexity you need to master before you can start working. Begin with simple Markdown files in a folder, and let your system evolve as your needs grow.
