## III) Dropbox

All Masterclass course materials will be shared via a Shared Folder on Dropbox.

You will be invited directly by me to join this Shared Folder via rj.vanspaandonk@imagineers.ai

### Dropbox Account & Shared Folder

You need a Dropbox account to access the Shared Folder. A free account is sufficient — you do not need a paid plan. We will send the Shared Folder invitation to the email address we have on file for you. What happens next depends on your situation:

**1\. You already have a Dropbox account, and it uses the same email address we have on file:**
You're all set. When you receive the invitation, simply click the link and add the folder to your Dropbox. Note: if you are on a free account, our materials may push you close to the 2 GB storage limit. If Dropbox prompts you to upgrade, there is no need to do so — simply free up some space by removing a few older files from your Dropbox.

**2\. You don't have a Dropbox account yet:**
You don't need to set one up in advance. When you receive our invitation email, click the link, and Dropbox will prompt you to create a free account. Use the email address we have on file for you, and select the free 2 GB Dropbox Basic Plan when prompted. Once your account is created, the Shared Folder will be added automatically.

If you prefer to set up your account ahead of time, you can do so at [https://www.dropbox.com/register](https://www.dropbox.com/register).

**3\. You already have a Dropbox account, but it uses a different email address than the one we have on file:**
Please let us know your Dropbox email address so we can send the invitation to the correct one. Alternatively, you can add the email address we have on file as a secondary email on your Dropbox account (under Account Settings > General > Secondary emails), and the invitation will reach your existing account.

### Dropbox Desktop Application

We also recommend that you install the Dropbox desktop APPLICATION on your machine. Information about this tool can be found here:

[https://www.dropbox.com/desktop](https://www.dropbox.com/desktop)

The main benefit is that it will automatically keep all the files that we will be sharing before, during and after the Masterclass up to date and protected (available for Windows and macOS). So, every time we add or change a file with new content, it will automatically sync to your machine, and you will always have the latest versions of the material.

**SO, EVEN THOUGH YOU CAN ACCESS ALL FILES VIA YOUR BROWSER WITH YOUR DROPBOX ACCOUNT, INSTALLING THE APP WOULD BE BENEFICIAL.**

You can download the application here:

[https://www.dropbox.com/install](https://www.dropbox.com/install)

The Dropbox desktop app is available for Windows (10 and 11) and macOS (Big Sur 11 and later).

#### Installing on macOS

1. Go to [https://www.dropbox.com/install](https://www.dropbox.com/install) and download the installer.
2. Open the downloaded file (DropboxInstaller.dmg) from your Downloads folder.
3. Double-click the Dropbox icon in the installer window that appears.
4. Follow the prompts to complete the installation.
5. Sign in with your Dropbox account credentials.

#### Installing on Windows

1. Go to [https://www.dropbox.com/install](https://www.dropbox.com/install) and download the installer.
2. Run the downloaded installer (DropboxInstaller.exe).
3. Follow the installation wizard instructions to complete the process.
4. Sign in with your Dropbox account credentials.

Once installed, two things will be added to your computer: a Dropbox folder in Finder (Mac) or File Explorer (Windows), and a Dropbox icon in your menu bar (Mac) or taskbar (Windows).

You may have the Dropbox app running on your machine already. If this is the case, you have nothing else to do in terms of installation.

PS: If you are using a corporate laptop, your IT department may restrict access to Dropbox or block the installation of the desktop application. Please check with your IT administrators beforehand.
